Thermal Transport Across Intra-Molecular Junctions Between Boron Nitride Nanotubes and Sheets

My specific contribution to this research focused on the development, implementation, and validation of custom simulation workflows to accurately model hexagonal boron nitride nanotube–nanosheet junctions using LAMMPS. I played a central role in coding and refining the atomic geometry construction, with particular emphasis on precise junction alignment between nanotubes and sheets. This alignment was critical to avoid artificial atomic overlap or local lattice mismatches that could introduce spurious phonon scattering or impedance, which would otherwise contaminate the thermal transport results. By ensuring registry-consistent junctions and stable bonded interfaces, the simulations reliably captured intrinsic phonon transport behavior rather than geometric artifacts. Using RNEMD-based thermal transport calculations under controlled mechanical deformation protocols, the results demonstrated that the thermal resistance of these hBN-based junction structures remains effectively unchanged under applied mechanical strain, indicating strong robustness of phonon transport pathways in properly aligned BNNT–hBN systems. Presented at ASME IMECE (Oral Presentation), ASME IMECE (Poster Presentation), KSU Fall Symposium (Oral Presentation). Publications in progress.
