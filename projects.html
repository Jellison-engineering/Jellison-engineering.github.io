BNNT–hBN Junction Thermal Transport Simulation

MATLAB LAMMPS RNEMD Nanomaterials

In this research, we investigate the effect of mechanical strain on the thermal transport in interfacial thermal resistance in pillared hexagonal boron nitride sheet structures using molecular dynamics simulations. More specifically, Reverse nonequilibrium molecular dynamics is used together with Tersoff force field. The results show that the interfacial thermal resistance across boron nitride nanotube (BNNT)-hBNNS is minimal when compared to typical interfacial thermal resistances found in traditional materials. Interfacial thermal resistance values become lowered when the pillar heights are longer. This indicates that the thermal transport in pillared hBNNS structures is still ballistic and phonon mode conversion through the junctions is very efficient. When the structure is strained, interfacial thermal resistance increases. This can be attributed to the deformed junctions with stretched interatomic bounds. Although the direction of the phonon transport becomes less radical while being deformed, the altered lattice structure by strain impact the phonon mode conversion across the junction more negatively, resulting in increased interfacial thermal resistance. The largest increase in interfacial thermal resistance was observed for the case with longest interplanar spacing because the longest interplanar spacing allowed for the largest deformation in the pillared hBNNS structure without breaking the junction.
